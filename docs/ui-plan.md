# Oversteer UI Plan

## Visual Direction

The interface should feel like editorial automotive media crossed with a high-end dashboard.

Visual cues:

- warm metal tones instead of generic blue or purple
- strong typography
- dark ink backgrounds with sand and copper accents
- full-screen cards and layered panels
- motion limited to meaningful transitions and feed snap behavior

## Screen 1: Onboarding

Goal:
Get a usable preference graph in under 60 seconds.

Sections:

- welcome statement
- interest chips
- brand selection
- era selection
- source-style preference
- notification preferences
- feed preview

## Screen 2: Feed

Goal:
Make the app immediately useful with a fullscreen vertical card stack.

Card anatomy:

- large media area
- source and time
- headline
- one-paragraph summary
- interest tags
- why-you-see-this reason
- action row for save, hide, less like this, follow

Behavior:

- vertical scroll like TikTok
- card snap aligns each story cleanly
- subtle transition between cards
- no cramped multi-column layouts

## Screen 2A: Pole Position

Goal:
Give the user a concise daily editorial briefing before or alongside the personalized lane.

Layout:

- lead story
- top trusted stories row
- race or launch spotlight
- jump into personalized lane

## Screen 3: Story Detail

Goal:
Provide depth without breaking trust.

Layout:

- hero image
- headline and metadata
- summary
- related coverage block
- source CTA to open original story
- reason for recommendation
- tags and follow controls

## Screen 4: Garage

Goal:
Give users a personal control center for their interests and saved material.

Sections:

- saved stories
- followed brands and topics
- watchlists
- recent reads
- source mix

## Screen 5: Pit Wall

Goal:
Show all relevant angles on one major story without flooding the main feed with duplicates.

Sections:

- lead coverage item
- official source or press release if available
- alternate takes
- timeline of updates
- related tags and follow controls

## Screen 6: Settings

Goal:
Let users tune the feed without making the app feel technical.

Sections:

- notifications
- source preferences
- muted topics
- language and region
- ranking style slider

## Navigation

Bottom nav:

- Feed
- Garage
- Settings

Onboarding should be a separate first-run flow, not a permanent main-tab destination.
