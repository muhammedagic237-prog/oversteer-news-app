# Oversteer Reference Patterns

This document captures the product patterns we should borrow from successful news apps without copying their visual identity or becoming a generic aggregator.

## What The Best Apps Get Right

### Google News

Useful pattern:

- explicit follow and unfollow controls for interests, sources, and locations
- "more or less like this" tuning through likes, unlikes, and hidden sources
- a "Full Coverage" pattern that groups multiple viewpoints around one story

Why it matters for Oversteer:

- car fans want control over brands, regions, and story types
- major stories like a new M3, Le Mans result, or Porsche pricing update need cluster views instead of duplicate cards

### Apple News

Useful pattern:

- editorial "Today" style entry point
- followed channels and topics appear more often
- favorite channels and topics create a sense of a personal newsroom

Why it matters for Oversteer:

- we should open with a daily editorial lane, not only a raw algorithmic stream
- users need a simple way to favorite brands, publications, or series

### Flipboard

Useful pattern:

- organizing stories around passions rather than one mixed feed
- separate, swipeable destinations for distinct interests
- a magazine-like visual treatment that feels curated rather than mechanical

Why it matters for Oversteer:

- "BMW", "Oldtimers", "Motorsport", and "Restomods" should feel like distinct lanes
- curation should feel like a hand-built enthusiast publication, not a cold ranking engine

### Feedly

Useful pattern:

- strong noise reduction through topic modeling and prioritization
- AI used for triage and filtering, not just summarization

Why it matters for Oversteer:

- the ranking engine should suppress weak rumors and repeated coverage
- AI should mainly enrich tags, clusters, and relevance, not replace source voice

### Inoreader

Useful pattern:

- user-owned feed control
- filters, rules, saved items, and source management

Why it matters for Oversteer:

- power users need source controls, muted topics, and saved story organization
- our "Garage" should feel like a personal command center

## What We Should Not Copy

- generic blue-and-white enterprise news layouts
- political-news information density
- cluttered multi-column desktop-first feed design
- black-box recommendation behavior with no explanation
- ad-heavy surfaces that weaken trust

## Oversteer Translation Layer

Successful pattern to adapt:

- Google News Full Coverage

Oversteer version:

- Pit Wall clusters that group launch coverage, tests, rumors, videos, and official announcements around one car story

Successful pattern to adapt:

- Apple News Today

Oversteer version:

- Pole Position, a daily editorial lane mixing the biggest trusted stories with user interest weighting

Successful pattern to adapt:

- Flipboard passions / magazines

Oversteer version:

- Lanes for BMW, JDM, Oldtimers, Motorsport, Auctions, and Restomods

Successful pattern to adapt:

- Feedly prioritization and filtering

Oversteer version:

- trust-weighted ranking, duplicate suppression, and rumor cooling

Successful pattern to adapt:

- Inoreader rules and organization

Oversteer version:

- Garage, source tuning, muted topics, and watchlists

## Product Direction We Keep

Oversteer should feel like:

- a high-trust enthusiast briefing
- a personalized car culture feed
- a fast swipe-first mobile product

It should not feel like:

- a generic RSS reader
- a social network clone
- a sensational rumor mill
