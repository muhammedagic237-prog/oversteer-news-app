# Oversteer Visual System

## Brand Mood

Oversteer should feel like:

- a midnight garage after a long drive
- an enthusiast magazine laid across a carbon dash
- warm metal, rubber, glass, and old leather

It should not feel like:

- a finance dashboard
- a politics newspaper
- a neon gamer interface

## Core Visual Principles

1. Editorial confidence
Big headlines, restrained metadata, clear hierarchy.

2. Mechanical warmth
Use copper, brass, sandstone, and graphite instead of generic bright blue.

3. Focused immersion
One primary story per screen in the main feed.

4. Texture over noise
Use gradients, subtle surface depth, and layered panels instead of busy decoration.

## Color System

Primary palette:

- Pit Black `#111315`
- Graphite `#24282d`
- Steel `#4d555e`
- Copper Accent `#d58b3d`
- Sand Text `#f3efe8`
- Dust Text `#b6aea1`

Extended moods:

- Heritage Sepia for classics and auctions
- Race Blue for motorsport content
- Alpine Silver for premium launches
- Track Red for major breaking alerts

## Typography Direction

Recommended pairing:

- headlines: a geometric grotesk with character
- body: a readable editorial serif or highly legible sans

The tone should be:

- strong
- premium
- readable at speed

Avoid default SaaS typography.

## Motion System

Main feed:

- strong vertical snap
- subtle parallax or light shift
- no exaggerated bounce

Supporting interactions:

- chip activation should feel tactile
- follow and save should have crisp state changes
- cluster expansion should feel like opening a dashboard compartment

## Surface System

### Pole Position

- editorial lead card
- high-trust badge
- morning briefing tone

### Your Lane

- fullscreen card stack
- dramatic imagery or gradients
- clear reason for recommendation

### Pit Wall

- multi-source comparison layout
- grouped article stack
- source diversity visible at a glance

### Garage

- denser but still premium
- tray and panel metaphors
- saved stories, watchlists, and source bundles

## Card Anatomy

Each primary card should contain:

- source pill
- publish time
- headline
- short summary
- tags
- why-you-see-this strip
- actions

Optional:

- cluster count
- trust tier
- event tag like "Launch", "Race", "Rumor", "Auction"

## Iconography

Use crisp, minimal icon shapes inspired by:

- instrument clusters
- route indicators
- flags
- pit wall signals

Avoid cartoonish car icons everywhere.

## Imagery Rules

- use source imagery when licensed or linked properly
- prioritize editorial photography, track shots, detail crops, and heritage archive visuals
- avoid overly glossy stock imagery

## Copy Tone

Labels and microcopy should sound:

- informed
- calm
- enthusiast-first

Examples:

- "Why you see this"
- "Trusted source"
- "Follow this lane"
- "Mute this topic"
- "Open full coverage"

## Design Differentiators

This app should stand apart by combining:

- the confidence of an editorial product
- the intimacy of a personal enthusiast notebook
- the speed of a swipe-first mobile feed

That blend is the unique flavor. The app should feel like your favorite car publication learned your taste and built you a private issue every day.
